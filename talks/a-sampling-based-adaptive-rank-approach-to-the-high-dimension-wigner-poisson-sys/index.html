A Sampling-Based Adaptive Rank Approach to the high-dimension Wigner-Poisson System

Track Kinetic Equations and Plasma Physics 2

Presenting speaker Sining Gong (Michigan State University)

Authors Andrew J. Chrsitlieb (Michigan State University); Sining Gong (Michigan State University); Jing-Mei Qiu (University of Delaware); Nanyi Zheng (University of Delaware)

Abstract

We present a novel, mass-conserving, sampling-based adaptive-rank solver that makes fully resolved 3D3V simulations of the Wigner-Poisson system tractable for the first time without relying on distributed computing. The Wigner-Poisson model is essential for capturing quantum uncertainty in applications ranging from semiconductor physics to warm dense matter in inertial confinement fusion (ICF)—such as the stopping power of alpha particles at the National Ignition Facility (NIF). While the equation reduces an $N^N$-body quantum problem to $N^6$ degrees of freedom via the BBGKY formalism, its extreme phase-space non-locality makes traditional parallelization computationally prohibitive. To overcome the curse of dimensionality, our approach leverages the insight that, unlike the classical Vlasov equation, Wigner-Poisson solutions naturally exhibit a low-rank structure in regimes of physical interest. We develop a structure-preserving adaptive-rank algorithm for high-dimensional cases. The resulting method achieves an optimal linear scaling in computation time. Furthermore, it strictly preserves mass density to machine precision and conserves momentum to the level of the employed adaptive-rank truncation.
