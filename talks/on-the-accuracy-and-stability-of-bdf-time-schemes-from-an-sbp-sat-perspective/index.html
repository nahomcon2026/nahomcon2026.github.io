On the accuracy and stability of BDF time schemes from an SBP-SAT perspective

Track Time integration 2

Presenting speaker Ayaboe K. Edoh (Amentum)

Authors Ayaboe K. Edoh (Amentum)

Abstract

The current work characterizes backwards differencing formula (BDF) methods as conservative differencing schemes in time. Decomposing the derivative operator according to structures pertaining to Summation-by-Parts and Simultaneous-Approximation-Terms (SBP-SATs) allows one to determine final-time energy stability of the solution based on the stencil coefficients, thus also accounting for the effect of start-up procedures. The SBP-SAT perspective is shown to identify implied quadrature rules and temporal diffusion effects associated with BDF progressions -- properties which are then used to algebraically characterize the accuracy and robustness of classical BDF schemes, as well as a new class of optimized energy-stable BDF2 methods to be presented.
