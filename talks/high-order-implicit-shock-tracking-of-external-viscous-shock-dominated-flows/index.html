High-Order Implicit Shock Tracking of External Viscous Shock-Dominated Flows

Track CFD 1

Presenting speaker Huijing Dong (University of Notre Dame)

Authors Huijing Dong (University of Notre Dame); Matthew J. Zahr (University of Notre Dame)

Abstract

High-order implicit shock tracking is a class of optimization-based numerical methods to approximate solutions of conservation laws with discontinuities by aligning elements of the computational mesh with non-smooth flow features. This alignment ensures that discontinuities are represented as inter-element jumps, while high-order basis functions accurately approximate smooth regions of the solution without the need for nonlinear stabilization, enabling accurate solutions on coarse meshes. In this talk, we present a robust numerical framework for viscous, shock-dominated flows at high Mach and Reynolds numbers based on implicit shock tracking and high-order discontinuous Galerkin discretizations. The proposed method employs inviscid implicit shock tracking to align the computational mesh with flow discontinuities, enabling accurate representation of shock structures. Once a shock-aligned mesh is obtained, upstream farfield elements are removed and the shock is treated as a computational boundary for subsequent viscous flow computations on the post-shock domain. This decoupled strategy improves robustness while avoiding severe mesh distortion often encountered in fully coupled viscous shock optimization. The effectiveness and flexibility of the framework are demonstrated through a set of hypersonic benchmark problems, yielding accurate predictions of surface pressure and heat flux profiles.
