Entropy stable high-order schemes for the compressible Navier--Stokes equations with isothermal no-slip wall boundary conditions

Track CFD 3

Presenting speaker Mohammed Sayyari (Old Dominion University)

Authors Mohammed Sayyari (Old Dominion University); Nail K. Yamaleev (Old Dominion University)

Abstract

Entropy-stable boundary conditions are critical for ensuring that the corresponding numerical scheme satisfies the discrete entropy inequality, thus mimicking the second law of thermodynamics for the compressible Navier-Stokes equations. Entropy-stable adiabatic wall boundary conditions were introduced by Parsani (2014). Then, further generalized by Dalcin (2019) to a moving adiabatic solid wall or a wall with a prescribed heat flux using summation-by-parts (SBP) operators. Also, a similar approach was used in Chan (2022) to impose adiabatic and isothermal no-slip wall boundary conditions for the modal Discontinuous Galerkin method. While the proposed isothermal no-slip wall boundary conditions in Chan (2022) mimic the continuous entropy inequality, they are not entropy-stable and invariably lead to positive entropy production. Moreover, to the best of the knowledge, no entropy-stable formulation for isothermal wall boundary conditions is currently available in the literature. This talk presents a new isothermal no-slip wall boundary condition that: 1) enforces the T(y=0)=T\_wall and v(y=0)=v\_wall conditions with design-order accuracy while maintaining stability, 2) provides the correct sign of entropy production, and 3) mimics the entropy balance of the Navier-Stokes equations at the discrete level. The structure-preserving and design-order properties of the proposed methodology will be demonstrated on standard benchmark problems for compressible flows.
