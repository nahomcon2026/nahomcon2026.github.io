High-Order Discontinuous Galerkin Methods from Action Principles

Track Wave propagation 1

Presenting speaker Thomas Hagstrom

Authors Thomas Hagstrom (Southern Methodist University); Jaryd Domine (Southern Methodist University); Stephen Lau (University of New Mexico)

Abstract

Most discontinuous Galerkin or summation-by-parts discretizations of hyperbolic equations are based on conservation laws. However, basic physical laws are more often presented as action principles. In this work we present a recipe for generating stable, arbitrary-order methods for simulating systems governed by action principles whose Euler-Lagrange equations are second-order in space-time and are of hyperbolic type. The essence of our approach is to introduce variables which are weakly equal to the time derivative of the fields and to automatically enforce an energy inequality on the discrete level. The existence of an appropriate energy is necessary for any system with finite speed of propagation, which underlies the generality of our approach. As a particular example we demonstrate applications to problems in gravitational wave theory. In addition we discuss issues related to gauge-fixing, methods, first proposed by Rothkopf and Nordstrom, which avoid forming the Euler-Lagrange equations entirely, and the most general form - the regularly-hyperbolic action principles analyzed by Christodoulou.
