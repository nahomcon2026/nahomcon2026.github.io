High-order Matrix-Free Spectral Density Estimation

Track Function approximation and sampling 2

Presenting speaker Andrew Horning (RPI)

Authors Jinjing Yi (Rutgers University, Flatiron Institute); Daniel Massatt (NJIT); Andrew Horning (RPI); Mitchell Luskin (University of Minnesota) Jed Pixley (Rutgers University, Flatiron Institute); Jason Kaye (Flatiron Institute)

Abstract

We introduce a new matrix-free algorithm for computing spectral densities of large \`\`mat-vec" matrices, and use it to compute the local density of states (LDOS) of sparse Hamiltonians derived from tight-binding models. Our approach can be viewed as a high-order variant of the popular regularized Chebyshev kernel polynomial method (KPM), which uses a high-order accurate approximation of the δ-function to achieve rapid convergence to the thermodynamic limit for smooth spectral densities. The computationally intensive steps are identical to those for KPM, and high-order accuracy is achieved with an inexpensive post-processing procedure. We apply the algorithm to tight-binding models of graphene and twisted bilayer graphene, demonstrating high-order convergence to the LDOS at non-singular points.
