Sampling in Function Approximation with Frames

Track Function approximation and sampling 2

Presenting speaker Rodrigo Barcelos Platte (ASU)

Authors Rodrigo Barcelos Platte (ASU)

Abstract

Sampling is crucial to the stability and accuracy of various global approximation methods, including polynomial and radial basis function methods. In this talk, I will explore how oversampling can be avoided in Frame approximation and introduce a class of greedy algorithms for generating effective sampling points. Additionally, I will discuss strategies to make these methods computationally efficient.
