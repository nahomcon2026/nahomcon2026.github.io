Multivariate Band Arnoldi (MBA) Method for Stable Function Approximation and Derivative Recovery

Track Function approximation and sampling 2

Presenting speaker Wen Jiang (Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University)

Authors Qiang Niu (Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University); Wen Jiang (Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University); Jinheng Wu (Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University)

Abstract

Multivariate polynomial least-squares approximation is notoriously ill-conditioned due to the exponential growth of the Vandermonde matrix condition number. Recent Krylov-subspace-based approaches, notably \\emph{Vandermonde with Arnoldi} (V+A) and Multivariate confluent Vandermonde with $G$-Arnoldi, have resolved the instability by replacing explicit ill-condioned system with orthogonalization on multiplication operators. In this paper, we extend these idea by introducing a Multivariate Band Arnoldi method for stable function approximation and unsupervised derivative recovery on arbitrary domains. By employing a FIFO queue-driven breadth-first search strategy, the orthogonalization process preserves the intrinsic multi-operator branching structure of multivariate polynomial spaces and deflation is applied to remove linearly dependent branches induced by operator commutativity. The proposed method constructs a discrete orthogonal polynomial basis on arbitrary point clouds and irregular domains. Through the coordinate bridge mechanism, we show that gradients and high-order derivatives can be reconstructed with high precision using only the function data. Numerical experiments on domains ranging from 2D disks to 4D hyperspheres demonstrate that the MBA framework achieves errors approaching machine precision for multivariate function approximation, while simultaneously recovering gradients and Laplacians with high precision. The proposed MBA method provides a stable and highly accurate framework for multivariate function approximation when derivative information is expensive or unavailable.
