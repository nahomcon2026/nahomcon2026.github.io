A DG Tensor-Train Approach for the Vlasov–Maxwell Equations

Track Kinetic Equations and Plasma Physics 1

Presenting speaker Rujeko Chinomona (Los Alamos National Laboratory)

Authors Rujeko Chinomona (Los Alamos National Laboratory), Dibyendu Adak (IIT-Kharagpur), Duc Truong (LANL), Kim Rasmussen (LANL), Boian Alexandrov (SLIC.AI)

Abstract

High-fidelity simulation of kinetic plasmas requires solving the Vlasov–Maxwell system, a high-dimensional set of equations describing the evolution of particle distribution functions coupled with electromagnetic fields. Standard discretizations suffer from the curse of dimensionality, making high-resolution simulations computationally demanding in both memory and runtime.In this work we investigate a framework that combines discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods with tensor-train (TT) decompositions for efficient numerical solution of the Vlasov–Maxwell equations. By representing the DG coefficient tensor in TT form, we reduce the storage and computational complexity while maintaining high-order accuracy in phase space. The approach enables efficient manipulation of the distribution function and facilitates scalable computations for kinetic plasma problems.We demonstrate the performance of the method on benchmark problems such as Landau damping and the streaming Weibel instability, highlighting both accuracy and compression efficiency. The results illustrate the potential of combining high-order discretizations with low-rank tensor representations to mitigate the curse of dimensionality in kinetic simulations.
