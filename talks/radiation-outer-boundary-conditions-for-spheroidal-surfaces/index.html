Radiation Outer Boundary Conditions for Spheroidal Surfaces

Track Wave propagation 2

Presenting speaker Stephen Lau (Mathematics and Statistics, University of New Mexico)

Authors Stephen Lau (Mathematics and Statistics, University of New Mexico)

Abstract

We consider wave simulation on a computational domain with an oblate spheroidal outer boundary. Exact outer boundary conditions feature nonlocal convolution in the time-domain, and we describe the corresponding kernel in the Laplace frequency domain and work on its rational approximation. Similar approximation (due to Alpert, Greengard, and Hagstrom) for a round-sphere outer boundary affords fast implementation of the corresponding temporal convolution. A chief complication in the spheroidal setting is a necessarily variable-coefficient normal vector field. Numerical experiments will rely on a modal, energy-preserving, discontinuous Galerkin scheme due to Appelo and Hagstrom, with further elaboration by Domine. Radiation conditions for prolate or oblate spheroidal surfaces in flat space (Minkowski spacetime) should have engineering applications. Further, they are mathematically similar to analogous conditions relevant for perturbations of spinning blackholes (Kerr spacetime).
