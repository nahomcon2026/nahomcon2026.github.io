Mapping Techniques for Obtaining High-Order Convergence for Problems with Singularities

Track High order accuracy and geometry

Presenting speaker Brian Helenbrook (Clarkson University)

Authors Brian Helenbrook (Clarkson University); Nojan Bagheri-Sadeghi (Clarkson University)

Abstract

Problems with singularities occur in almost every geometry which has an obtuse or acute corner. These singularities can destroy the high-order convergence that one hopes to obtain using a high-order method. Several different techniques have been proposed to overcome this difficulty including introducing specialized basis functions, mesh adaptation techniques, and mapping techniques. The last two techniques are inter-related in that a uniform mesh in a mapped domain becomes an adapted mesh in the physical domain. However, mapping is more general in that it also changes the space in which the polynomial interpolants approximate the solution. In this presentation, we examine several different mapping techniques and determine the benefits and drawbacks of the various techniques in terms of accuracy, ease of implementation, and computational efficiency. The various techniques are applied to singular problems in fluids and solid mechanics such as flow over an airfoil and a corner singularity to demonstrate the ability to obtain high-order accuracy for practical problems.
