PI-RSNs: Physics Informed Randomized Spectral Networks

Track ML and data-driven modeling

Presenting speaker Jose Agudelo Velez (UNM)

Authors Jose Agudelo Velez (UNM), Mohammad Motamed (UNM)

Abstract

We introduce a novel physics-informed neural network (PINN) framework for approximating solutionsof second-order elliptic boundary value problems in d ≥ 1 spatial dimensions. The framework exploits thestructure of randomized spectral networks (RSNs), with parameters encompassing frequency modes thatare sampled from a theoretically-driven optimal distribution and amplitudes that are obtained by solvinga least squares problem. In this talk, we define spectral networks, explain what it means for frequencyparameters to be “randomized” via our novel training approach in the solution-data setting, and givean overview of how they are trained given sampled evaluations of the interior and boundary differentialoperators. Several numerical experiments, ranging from the steady advection–diffusion equation to theHelmholtz equation, will be presented, followed by a discussion of the broader class of problems towhich we anticipate this method may be applied. We also address numerical and theoretical challengesencountered, along with proposed solutions. Finally, we compare the proposed technique with existingPINN strategies and discuss which challenges overlap and which this method handles well.
