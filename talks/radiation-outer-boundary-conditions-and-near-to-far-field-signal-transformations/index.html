Radiation Outer Boundary Conditions And Near-to-far Field Signal Transformations For The Bardeen-Press Equation

Track Wave propagation 1

Presenting speaker Som Dev Bishoyi (UMass Dartmouth)

Authors Som Dev Bishoyi (UMass Dartmouth)Scott Field (UMass Dartmouth)Stephen Lau (University of New Mexico)

Abstract

A wide range of theoretical and astrophysical problems – including gravitational-wave modeling for extreme mass-ratio inspirals – require accurate time-domain solutions of the spin-weight s = −2 Teukolsky equation in Boyer-Lindquist coordinates. Because such simulations are performed on finite computational domains, they typically introduce an artificial outer boundary where nontrivial boundary conditions must be imposed. If these conditions are inaccurate, long-time evolutions can be corrupted by spurious reflections and slowly growing unphysical modes that contaminate the simulation. We develop and implement exact radiation outer boundary conditions (ROBCs) for the Bardeen-Press equation (the a = 0 limit of the Teukolsky equation), making the artificial boundary fully transparent at any finite radius. We also construct near-to-far-field teleportation kernels that map data recorded at finite radius to the waveform at future null infinity. We show that both the boundary and teleportation kernels can be accurately approximated by sums of exponentials, together with associated error bounds. Implemented in a time-domain solver, our kernel-based boundary treatments eliminate unphysical late-time growth, reproduce the correct late-time decay rates, and enable efficient long-duration simulations relevant to waveform modeling and related black-hole perturbation calculations.
