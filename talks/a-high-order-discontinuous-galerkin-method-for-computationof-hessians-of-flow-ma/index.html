A High-Order Discontinuous Galerkin Method for Computationof Hessians of Flow Maps

Track CFD 6

Presenting speaker Sergio A. Castiblanco-Ballesteros (San Diego State University)

Authors Sergio A. Castiblanco-Ballesteros (San Diego State University); Gustaaf B. Jacobs (San Diego State University)

Abstract

Discontinuous Galerkin spectral element method (DG-SEM) solvers for the Navier–Stokes equations compute Eulerian velocity fields with high-order spatial accuracy. Increasingly, Lagrangian diagnostics based on flow maps and their gradients, such as finite-time Lyapunov exponents (FTLE) and material-sheet curvature, are used to visualize coherent flow structures. A DG-SEM-consistent algorithm is presented to compute flow-map gradients and Hessians without degrading accuracy. In the first stage, particles are seeded at quadrature nodes and advected with the DG-SEM solver over a finite-time interval. The advected elemental solution encodes the flow map while preserving the piecewise polynomial basis. In the second stage, flow-map gradients and Hessians are evaluated using DG-SEM element operators and high-order interpolation. This two-stage workflow avoids the memory and I/O burden of storing densely sampled velocity snapshots, which would otherwise be required to achieve comparable accuracy. In examples, we illustrate how the flow-map gradients quantify finite-time deformation: eigenvectors give principal stretching directions, and eigenvalues identify material barriers and coherent Lagrangian structures. Likewise, the flow-map Hessian captures nonlinear spatial variation of deformation and provides directional measures of bending and folding, relevant to stability diagnostics and finite-time separation indicators.
