A comparison of mapping and mesh adaptation methods in problems containing singularities

Track Finite element methods

Presenting speaker Nojan Bagheri-Sadeghi (Clarkson University)

Authors Nojan Bagheri-Sadeghi (Clarkson University); Brian T. Helenbrook (Clarkson University)

Abstract

The expected convergence rate of high-order finite element methods is lost in the presence of singularities, which often occur at corners in the geometry. Two approaches used to recover the optimal convergence rate are mesh adaptation and mapping methods. In mesh adaptation methods, non-uniform mesh adaptation is used to recover the optimal convergence rates in terms of number of unknowns used to resolve the solution. In mapping methods, the solution is mapped into a coordinate system where it is regular and uniform mesh refinement is applied in this coordinate system. This study compares different mappings and mesh adaptation techniques for h-FEM methods (i.e., methods with a uniform degree of basis functions over all elements). For such FEMs, a radical mesh is shown to recover the expected order of accuracy and minimize the error near singularities with minimal degrees of freedom. Here, we propose a logarithmic mapping that can recover the expected order of accuracy even in applications where mesh adaptation may fail. The convergence behavior in the mean-square and maximum error norms for multiple case studies near singularities is studied. The convergence behavior of the proposed mapping is compared with other mapping techniques and a radial mesh adaptation.
