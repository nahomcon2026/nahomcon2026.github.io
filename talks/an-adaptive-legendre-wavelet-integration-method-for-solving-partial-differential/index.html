An Adaptive Legendre Wavelet Integration Method for Solving Partial Differential Equations

Track CFD 1

Presenting speaker Catherine Mavriplis (University of Ottawa)

Authors Catherine Mavriplis (University of Ottawa); Aktham Mansi (University of Ottawa)

Abstract

Partial differential equations (PDEs) governing complex physical phenomena such as incompressible flows often require multiscale numerical approaches to tackle the breadth of physical features that develop over time and to produce highly accurate solutions within reasonable timeframes. Past work in hp-adaptive spectral Galerkin methods has led us to consider wavelets as an alternative to polynomials to capitalize on their multiresolution properties. Our recent work established the framework for the Legendre Wavelet Integration Method, in which higher-order derivatives of PDEs are expanded using Legendre wavelet bases, and the solution is constructed through integration. Integration leads to boundary conditions appearing explicitly in the algebraic system. We now add adaptive capabilities through the use of modal-based a posteriori error estimators that identify regions requiring better resolution while providing criteria to decide between refinement in the number and placement of intervals and increasing the polynomial order within the wavelet approach. The adaptive wavelet framework simplifies the overhead in the hp-adaptive spectral Galerkin method associated to the parent-child element topologies and the necessary mortar implementation for nonconforming interfaces, thanks to the global multiresolution approach of wavelets. Adaptive wavelet solutions of various PDEs including the Navier-Stokes equations are presented to demonstrate the efficiency of the approach.
