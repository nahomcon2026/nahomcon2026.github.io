Performance Study of Half-Closed Discontinuous Galerkin Schemes for Turbulent Flow Problems

Track CFD 2

Presenting speaker Per-Olof Persson (University of California, Berkeley)

Authors Per-Olof Persson (University of California, Berkeley); Yulong Pan (University of California, Berkeley)

Abstract

We present a performance study of the newly proposed Half-Closed Discontinuous Galerkin (HCDG) scheme \[1\] in the context of high-order accurate turbulent flow simulations. This method utilizes half-closed nodes, which improve the efficiency of assembling DG operators and enhance quadrature accuracy compared to traditional Gauss-Lobatto nodes. We investigate the nonlinear stability benefits resulting from the higher integration order, as well as the computational cost advantages over a fully integrated DG scheme. Additionally, we discuss novel linear solver techniques based on elimination, demonstrating how classical static condensation can be applied to a discontinuous Galerkin discretization. We also examine various solvers for the resulting linear system, including ILU-based smoothers and multigrid methods. Our preliminary results indicate speedups of 5–10x due to the smaller matrices and the improved spectral properties of the eliminated operators. We validate these findings through several benchmark problems in high-order CFD simulations, including Large Eddy Simulations of under-resolved turbulent flows.\[1\] Y. Pan, P.-O. Persson, "Half-closed discontinuous Galerkin discretisations," J. Comput. Phys., Volume 524, 1 March 2025, 113731.
