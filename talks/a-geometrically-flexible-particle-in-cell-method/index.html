A Geometrically Flexible Particle-in-Cell Method

Track Kinetic Equations and Plasma Physics 1

Presenting speaker Stephen White (LANL)

Authors Stephen White (LANL); Andrew Christlieb (MSU)

Abstract

Previously we have presented a new PIC method for the Vlasov-Maxwell system in which the fields were cast in terms of vector and scalar potentials using the Lorenz gauge. The waves are solved using the Method of Lines Transpose, and the particles are updated using a modification of the Asymmetrical Euler Method. The wave solver in particular displays a number of benefits including high order accuracy in space, access to a variety of boundary conditions, and gradients of equal order accuracy. This method was promised to have inherit geometric flexibility, which we now will exhibit with a number of numerical experiments.
