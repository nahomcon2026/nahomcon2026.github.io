Efficient, decoupled, second and third-order Implicit-Explicit Runge-Kutta schemes with original-form energy stability for the anisotropic phase-field dendritic growth model

Track Time integration 2

Presenting speaker Zhaoqing Xu (University of South Carolina)

Authors Jianan Li (Southern University of Science and Technology), Zhaoqing Xu (University of South Carolina), Jiang Yang (Southern University of Science and Technology), Xiaofeng Yang (University of South Carolina)

Abstract

We study numerical approximations for the anisotropic phase-field model of dendritic crystal growth, a strongly nonlinear system coupling the anisotropic Allen–Cahn equation with the heat equation through latent heat effects. Constructing fully decoupled schemes with provable energy stability under the original energy remains a major challenge.We develop a novel linear, high-order (second- and third-order) fully decoupled implicit–explicit Runge–Kutta framework. The proposed schemes preserve the original energy stability under a suitable Lipschitz modification of the double-well potential, without altering the energy structure. The key ingredients include stabilization techniques and carefully designed coefficient matrices for different nonlinear terms. We establish practical conditions on these coefficients and introduce a search strategy to construct admissible schemes, together with rigorous energy stability proofs.Extensive 2D and 3D simulations demonstrate the stability, efficiency, and accuracy of the proposed methods.
