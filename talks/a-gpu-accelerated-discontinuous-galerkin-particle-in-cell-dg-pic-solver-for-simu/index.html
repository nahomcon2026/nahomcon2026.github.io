A GPU-Accelerated Discontinuous Galerkin Particle-in-Cell (DG-PIC) Solver for Simulations of Plasmas

Track Kinetic Equations and Plasma Physics 2

Presenting speaker Gustaaf Jacobs (San Diego State University)

Authors Tristan Montoya (HyperComp), Nigel Nunn (Independent Researcher), Tim Warburton (Virginia Tech), Vijaya Shankar (HyperComp), William Hall (HyperComp), Gustaaf Jacobs (San Diego State University)

Abstract

A GPU-accelerated particle-in-cell solver for plasma simulations is presented, employing a nodal discontinuous Galerkin approximation of Maxwell's equations on unstructured triangular meshes \\cite{HW02} coupled with a Lagrangian particle method \\cite{JH06}. Charged particles are traced in their Lagrangian frame and coupled to the time-domain field solver using high-order interpolation and a charge-conserving deposition scheme. Gauss' law is enforced through a hyperbolic divergence cleaning method based on a Lagrange multiplier constraint. To alleviate stiffness, we use an IMEX-SDIRK method \\cite{JH09} to solve Maxwell's equations with hyperbolic divergence cleaning implicitly while advancing particles explicitly. The solver supports configurable arithmetic precision, both in its use of NVIDIA's cuDSS direct sparse solver for the implicit field solve and in its use of optimized CUDA kernels for explicit particle tracking. The performance of the GPU code with mixed 64-bit/32-bit precision is assessed through simulations of the Weibel instability and a planar sheath problem. A speedup by a factor of more than 20 is achieved for the CUDA-accelerated solver on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5060 Ti GPU relative to a serial CPU implementation on an Intel Core i9-13900HK, rendering practical simulations feasible.
