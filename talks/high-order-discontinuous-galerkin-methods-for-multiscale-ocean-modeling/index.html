High-Order Discontinuous Galerkin Methods for Multiscale Ocean Modeling

Track CFD 4

Presenting speaker Michal A. Kopera (Boise State University)

Authors Michal A. Kopera (Boise State University), Yao Gahounzo (Florida State University)

Abstract

We will present a high-order Galerkin framework for the numerical solution of multi-layer shallow water models, motivated by the need for accurate and robust simulation of multiscale ocean flows. The approach is based on unstructured meshes and variable-order polynomial expansions, leading to arbitrarily high-order discontinuous Galerkin numerical schemes. The goal is to conduct a systematic investigation of the accuracy-resolution trade-off, comparing various polynomial approximations with traditional finite-difference/finite-volume methods used in ocean modeling. Using the classical double-gyre problem as a testbed, we compare qualitative and quantitative diagnostics—including kinetic energy evolution and flow structure.The results illustrate that higher-order methods achieve greater fidelity per degree of freedom than lower-order schemes, while naturally accommodating local refinement. Particular attention is given to the role of numerical dissipation, the interpretation of long-time averaged quantities, and the sensitivity of diagnostics to discretization choices. I will also discuss algorithmic and analytical challenges that arise when extending these methods to realistic ocean settings, including stability and wetting and drying.
